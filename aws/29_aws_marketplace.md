# AWS Marketplace

## What It Is

AWS Marketplace is an online store where customers can find, buy, and deploy software that runs on AWS. Sellers can also list and sell their own solutions.

## How It Works

**As a buyer**, you browse and subscribe to software (AMIs, SaaS, containers, ML models) and pay through your AWS bill.

**As a seller**, you register, submit your product for review, and list it for global distribution:

- **Registration requirements**: AWS account, seller registration, tax/banking information
- **Sellable formats**: AMI, SaaS, container, ML model (SageMaker), SaaS API-based agents
- **Pricing models**: Hourly, monthly, usage-based, BYOL (Bring Your Own License)
- **Seller eligibility**: Must be a resident/citizen of eligible countries or a business entity established in one

## Example

A company with a proprietary AI document-processing solution packages it as a SageMaker model and lists it on AWS Marketplace. Customers subscribe and pay per inference call, with billing handled through their existing AWS account.

## Why It Matters

Marketplace provides a global distribution channel with built-in AWS billing integration, reducing the friction of selling and buying cloud software. For buyers, it simplifies procurement and consolidates costs into a single AWS invoice.
